# Voice, Structure & Style Reference

## Audience Context

- ~120 people, primarily friends and family (not institutional VCs)
- Stitchi is bootstrapped — no board, no term sheets, no fiduciary reporting obligation
- These updates are about relationship-building, sharing the journey, and staying top of mind
- Readers are rooting for Everest — they want to help and feel connected to the progress
- Tone is a personal letter from a founder to people who believe in the company

## Everest's Voice

Based on 5 quarters of actual updates. Write as Everest would:
- Personal and warm in the opening — shares genuine reflections, moments of candor
- Grounded optimism: confident without overselling, lets numbers speak
- Transparent about challenges, always paired with what's being done
- Appreciative of the network without being sappy
- Uses "we" for the team, "I" for personal reflections
- Casual touches work (humor in Q4'24 opener about hangover pills, pizza boxes reference in Q3'25)

### Tone calibration
| ✅ Sounds like Everest | ❌ Doesn't sound like Everest |
|---|---|
| "If I had to summarize Q4 in one line: we're building a machine." | "We're thrilled to share incredible momentum!" |
| "We lost the deal, and the biggest learning was about positioning" | "There are some headwinds in the market" |
| "I'm more energized than ever" | "The team is ecstatic about our trajectory" |
| "What started as a scrappy effort has evolved into something more structured" | "We've undergone a transformational journey" |
| "Those moments remind me why we started this in the first place" | "Our passion continues to drive innovation" |

### Anti-patterns to avoid
- Hype words: breakthrough, revolutionary, massive, incredible, game-changing
- Hedge phrases: "We believe that…", "It seems like…"
- Corporate investor-speak: burn rate, runway, dilution, TAM
- Vague attributions: "The team has been working hard on…"
- Em dash overuse (max 1–2 per update)
- "Rule of three" bullet padding
- Negative parallelisms: "not just X, but Y"

## Document Envelope

Every update starts with:
```
Subject: Stitchi Investor Update [Q{N} {YYYY}]

This is a private list of Stitchi's investors, closest friends, and family. Please hold these updates in confidence & do not share. If you no longer want to receive updates, reply and let me know. Inversely, let me know if you want me to add someone on your team that I missed.
```

Salutation: `Dear Friends, Family, and Investors,` or `Dear all,`

Sign-off: Rotate naturally. Past closings: "Onward,", "Best,", "Onward and upward,", "To 2026 and beyond,"

Always sign: `Everest Guerra`

## Standard Structure (with emoji headers)

### 1. Opening Narrative
1–3 paragraphs. This is where personality lives. Think letter, not memo.

Patterns from actual updates:
- Lead with a one-line summary or a moment of personal reflection
- Reference the journey, team, milestones — what the quarter *felt* like
- Can include humor, vulnerability, gratitude
- End the opening by transitioning to the data: "Below is our Q4 snapshot and what we're focused on next." or "Read on for what we accomplished, what we learned, and what's ahead."

### 2. ⌛ tl;dr
Key metrics, period-over-period. Always include QoQ delta as percentage.

**Core recurring KPIs** (based on all 5 historical updates):

| Metric | Appeared in | Status |
|---|---|---|
| Revenue | All 5 quarters | Always include |
| Net New Logos / Net New ICP Logos | Q3'25, Q4'25 | Include (replaced Order Count) |
| Account Signups | All 5 quarters | Include |
| Organic Clicks | All 5 quarters | Include |
| Website Traffic | Q1'25, Q2'25, Q3'25 | Dropped in Q4'25 — include if interesting |
| Gross Margin | Q4'24, Q1'25 | Dropped after Q1'25 — include if notable |
| Order Count | Q4'24, Q1'25, Q2'25 | Replaced by Net New Logos in Q3 |

**Recommended standard set going forward:**
- Revenue: $X (+Y% QoQ)
- Net New Logos: X (+Y%)
- Account Signups: X (+Y%)
- Organic Clicks: X (+Y%)

Include Website Traffic and Gross Margin only when they tell a material story.

**Format note:** Use the exact tl;dr format from historical updates — metric name, colon, value, parenthetical delta. Don't over-format.

### 3. ⭐ Wins & Highlights
Organized by theme (Team, Go-to-Market, Operations, Customer, etc.). Each item is a short paragraph (1–3 sentences), not a one-liner. Use sub-labels in bold when grouping by theme.

Typically 4–8 items. Priority: customer wins > team growth > operational improvements > community/ecosystem.

### 4. 🎓 Challenges & Learnings
2–4 items. Each has:
- **Bold label:** The challenge in 2–3 words
- Paragraph explaining what happened and what's being done about it

This section earns credibility. Be specific — name the deal, name the issue, name the learning. Everest doesn't do vague challenges.

### 5. 🚀 Up Next
3–6 concrete priorities for the next 1–2 quarters. Each is a short paragraph with a clear action, not just a category. Can include hiring, GTM, product, operations.

### 6. ❓ Asks
3–4 specific, actionable asks. Historical patterns:
- **Warm Introductions:** Always include. Specify role, company size, revenue threshold.
- **Partner/Referral:** When actively building channel partnerships.
- **Hiring:** When actively recruiting.
- **Feedback/Office Visits:** Soft ask to maintain engagement.

Every ask should answer: "What exactly should this person do this week?"

## Formatting Rules

- **Plain text** — no markdown headers in the final output (Notion renders its own styling)
- **Emoji section headers** (⌛, ⭐, 🎓, 🚀, ❓) — this is Everest's established pattern
- **Numbers:** Always use specific numbers ("$262,548" not "~$260k"). QoQ deltas as percentages.
- **Bolding:** Use for theme labels in Wins section and challenge labels in Challenges section.
- **Length:** 700–1200 words (historical range). Q3'25 was the longest (~1100 words); Q4'24 was shortest (~700 words).
- **No tables** in the update body.

---

## Cadence

Quarterly only. Published within first 2–3 weeks of the following quarter.

Title format in Notion: `{YYYY} Q{N}` (e.g., "2026 Q1")
Subject line in email: `Stitchi Investor Update [Q{N} {YYYY}]`

## Data the Author Provides Manually

The following data points are NOT available from automated sources and must be provided by Everest:
- Revenue (exact figure from accounting)
- Gross Margin (if including)
- Account Signups (from Stitchi platform)
- Website Traffic / Organic Clicks (from analytics)
- Narrative framing / one-line summary
- "Up Next" priorities
- Specific asks
- Any deals, hires, or milestones not captured in Pipedrive/Front
