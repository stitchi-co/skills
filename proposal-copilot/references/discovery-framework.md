# Discovery Framework

Before drafting any proposal, gather these inputs. The quality of custom sections (exec summary, personas) directly depends on the depth of discovery.

## Required Inputs (must have before drafting)

1. **Company name** — full legal name + preferred short name
2. **Primary contact** — name, title, email
3. **Industry / vertical** — determines persona selection and language
4. **Employee count & locations** — shapes program scope and logistics
5. **Program type** — company store, recurring kits, event support, or combination
6. **Core need** — what triggered the search? (new program, replacing incumbent, scaling existing)

## High-Value Inputs (significantly improve proposal quality)

7. **Specific pain points** — what's broken or missing today?
8. **Current vendor** (if any) — who, what's working, what's not
9. **Key stakeholder groups** — who uses merch and for what purposes?
10. **Budget range or annual spend target** — helps with tier recommendation and ROI framing
11. **Decision timeline** — urgency shapes proposal pacing and language
12. **Decision criteria** — what matters most? (price, quality, speed, service, technology)
13. **Compliance requirements** — SSO mandates, procurement rules, approval workflows

## Context Sources (check these for intel)

- **CRM** — deal record, notes, custom fields (account tier, opportunity type)
- **Email / helpdesk** — threads with the prospect
- **Meeting notes** — transcripts from discovery calls
- **LinkedIn** — company size, recent news, hiring patterns (signals growth)
- **Client's website** — about page, careers page (for org structure hints), press releases

## Discovery Questions (for live calls or to guide email)

### Program Goals
- "What does a successful merch program look like for you in year one?"
- "What's driving the timing — is there a launch date or event?"
- "How do you measure the success of your current merch/swag?"

### Current State
- "How are you handling branded merchandise today?"
- "What's working well? What would you change?"
- "How much time does your team spend coordinating merch?"

### Stakeholders & Use Cases
- "Who are the main groups that receive or interact with branded merchandise?"
- "What events or moments drive merch needs throughout the year?"
- "Are there safety/compliance requirements for any of your apparel?"

### Technology & Process
- "Do your employees currently shop from an online store, or is it request-based?"
- "Do you need SSO, approval workflows, or budget controls?"
- "How do you track inventory and spending today?"

### Budget & Decision
- "Do you have a budget range in mind for the program?"
- "Who else is involved in this decision?"
- "Are you evaluating other vendors?"

## Mapping Discovery to Proposal Sections

| Discovery Input | Proposal Section Impacted |
|---|---|
| Pain points | Executive Summary (lead with these) |
| Stakeholder groups | Personas & Merch Matrix |
| Industry / vertical | Persona naming, merch ideas, case study selection |
| Budget range | Pricing tier recommendation, build package selection |
| Current vendor | Exec Summary (transition framing), Service Commitment |
| Compliance needs | Store Features emphasis, Security section |
| Employee count | Fulfillment scope, warehousing recommendations |
| Decision criteria | Section ordering (put highest-weight criteria earlier) |
