# Stakeholder Persona Library

Stakeholder personas describe the **end users** of the client's merch program — the people who will interact with the store, receive products, or benefit from branded merchandise. They are NOT buyer personas.

Each proposal should include 4–6 personas tailored to the client's organization. Use these archetypes as starting points, then customize based on what's known about the client.

## Persona Format

Each persona has:
- **Name & Role** — Alliterative name + functional title (e.g., "Employee Emma — Office/Admin, stipend shopper")
- **Events** — When/where this persona interacts with merch (2–4 scenarios)
- **Merch Ideas** — Specific product categories that fit this persona (4–6 items)

## Tailoring Guide

When building personas for a specific client:
1. **Start with what you know** about their org — employee types, locations, industries served, events they run
2. **Pick 4–6 archetypes** that match their actual stakeholder groups
3. **Rename** to fit their world (e.g., "Field Frank" → "Crew Member Carlos" for a construction company)
4. **Customize events** to their actual calendar (safety stand-downs, annual conference, quarterly all-hands)
5. **Customize merch** to their brand/industry norms and budget level
6. **Add a "Quick Takeaways" summary** beneath the table grouping personas by theme

## Quick Takeaways Format

Below the persona table, add 2–3 sentence summaries grouped by theme:
> **Employees** → Stipend-friendly, professional yet wearable everyday.
> **Field teams** → Rugged, safety-focused, weather-ready.
> **Clients** → Premium, tasteful, relationship-driven.
