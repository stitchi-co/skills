## What Are Content Pillars

Content pillars are 3-5 recurring themes that structure everything you post. They ensure:
- Consistency in topic (audience knows what to expect)
- Coverage across the content funnel (TOFU/MOFU/BOFU)
- Authentic alignment with founder expertise
- ICP relevance (every post serves a purpose)

Choose pillars at the intersection of: **what you know deeply** + **what your ICP cares about** + **what differentiates you**.

## Pillar Templates by Business Type

### SaaS / Platform Founder
1. Industry POV / contrarian takes
2. Build-in-public (metrics, milestones, lessons)
3. Customer success stories
4. Product/technical insights
5. Leadership/team culture

### Services / Agency Founder
1. Behind-the-scenes operations
2. Client transformation stories (anonymized if needed)
3. Industry trends + your take
4. Process/framework sharing
5. Hiring and team building

### Marketplace / E-commerce Founder
1. Supply chain / ops insights
2. Customer/user stories
3. Market trends + analysis
4. Build-in-public (growth metrics)
5. Category expertise (become THE expert in your niche)

## Stitchi-Specific Pillars

Tailored for Stitchi's positioning as branded merchandise platform. Adjust as strategy evolves.

### Pillar 1: Merch Operations Demystified (MOFU — 30%)
The unsexy reality of branded merchandise at scale. Sourcing, inventory, fulfillment, quality.
- "Here's what actually happens when you order 5,000 branded hoodies"
- "The hidden cost of bad merch ops nobody talks about"
- Supply chain insights, vendor management, quality control stories

### Pillar 2: Brand & Culture Through Merchandise (TOFU — 20%)
Why merch matters for employer brand, marketing, and culture.
- "The onboarding kit that increased 90-day retention by 15%"
- "Why your swag closet is costing you more than you think"
- Broader takes on employee experience, brand perception, workplace culture

### Pillar 3: Build-in-Public / Founder Journey (TOFU — 15%)
Everest's journey building Stitchi — real numbers, real lessons, real mistakes.
- Revenue milestones, team growth, product decisions
- "What I got wrong about X in year 1"
- Fundraising, hiring, pivots

### Pillar 4: Customer Wins & Transformations (BOFU — 20%)
How real companies solved real merch problems.
- Before/after stories (manual → automated)
- Scale stories ("How [company] went from 2 vendors to 1 partner")
- ROI and efficiency metrics

### Pillar 5: Tech + Ops Intersection (MOFU — 15%)
Where software meets real-world operations — Stitchi's sweet spot.
- How tech reduces manual merch steps
- Integration stories (connecting systems that don't naturally talk)
- "Why merch programs fail: it's a software problem, not a people problem"

## Content Calendar Template

### Weekly Cadence (5 posts/week)

| Day | Funnel | Pillar | Format |
|-----|--------|--------|--------|
| Monday | MOFU | Merch Ops Demystified | Text + image |
| Tuesday | TOFU | Brand & Culture / Personal story | Text only |
| Wednesday | MOFU | Tech + Ops Intersection | Carousel or text + image |
| Thursday | BOFU | Customer Wins | Text + image (screenshot/data) |
| Friday | TOFU | Build-in-Public / Industry take | Text only |

Rotate and experiment. This is a starting template — iterate based on what performs.

### Monthly Specials
- 1x deep-dive newsletter article or long-form post
- 1x team/hiring spotlight
- 1x poll (audience research or conversation starter)
- 1x reshare of industry content with founder commentary

## Voice Guide

### Founder Voice (Everest's Account)
- **Tone:** Direct, knowledgeable, approachable. Not corporate, not bro-y.
- **Perspective:** Operator who's in the trenches, not a thought leader lecturing from above.
- **Authenticity markers:** Specific details, real numbers, admitting mistakes, naming the messy parts.
- **Avoid:** Buzzwords ("synergy," "leverage," "innovative"), generic motivation, humble-bragging.
- **Format preferences:** Short paragraphs. Whitespace. One idea per sentence when making a point.

### Company Voice (Stitchi Page)
- **Tone:** Professional but warm. Expert but accessible.
- **Perspective:** The platform/team, not an individual.
- **Best for:** Product announcements, case studies, industry reports, job postings, event recaps.
- **Avoid:** Being a ghost town — post minimum 2x/week. Don't just reshare founder posts.
