## Content Funnel Framework

Every content calendar should balance three layers:

| Layer | Purpose | Mix | Example |
|-------|---------|-----|---------|
| TOFU (Top) | Attract attention, cast wide net | 20% | Personal story, industry hot take, lessons learned |
| MOFU (Middle) | Cement authority with ICP | 60% | How-to breakdowns, industry analysis, frameworks, behind-the-scenes |
| BOFU (Bottom) | Convert followers to pipeline | 20% | Case studies, feature highlights, customer wins, CTAs |

## Hook Patterns

The hook (first 1-2 lines before "see more") determines 80% of a post's performance. Patterns that work:

### 1. Bold Claim / Contrarian Take
```
Most companies get [X] completely wrong.
Here's what actually works:
```

### 2. Specific Number / Metric
```
We went from $0 to $2M ARR in 14 months.
Here's the unglamorous truth of how:
```

### 3. Story Opener
```
Last week a customer told me something that changed how I think about [X].
```

### 4. Pattern Interrupt
```
WARNING: this is a long post.
But if you're a [role] doing [thing], it might save you [outcome].
```

### 5. Confession / Vulnerability
```
I almost shut down the company 6 months ago.
Not because of revenue. Because of [unexpected reason].
```

### 6. List Promise
```
5 things I wish I knew before [experience].
(#3 cost me $50K)
```

### 7. Question Hook
```
Why do 90% of [X] fail in the first year?
I think the answer is simpler than people make it.
```

### 8. Build-in-Public Update
```
Monthly update 🧵
Revenue: $X (+Y% MoM)
Team: X people
Biggest lesson this month:
```

## Post Formats

### Text-Only Post
Best for: hot takes, personal stories, lessons learned.
Length: 150-300 words (sweet spot). Can go longer for deep dives.
Structure:
```
[Hook — 1-2 lines]

[Space / line break]

[Body — story, framework, or argument]
[Use short paragraphs, 1-3 sentences each]
[Whitespace between paragraphs]

[Closing — takeaway, question, or CTA]
```

### Text + Image
Best for: screenshots, data charts, before/after, behind-the-scenes photos.
The image should add proof or context the text can't convey alone.
Avoid generic stock photos.

### Carousel (PDF Document)
Best for: listicles, step-by-step guides, frameworks, breakdowns.
Tips:
- 6-12 slides is the sweet spot
- First slide = hook (treat it like a post hook)
- One idea per slide
- Large text, minimal design
- Last slide = CTA or summary

### Text + Video
Best for: product demos, founder talks, behind-the-scenes clips.
Keep under 90 seconds for feed videos. Captions mandatory (most watch muted).

### Poll
Best for: engagement spikes, audience research, conversation starters.
Use sparingly — max 1/week. Make options genuinely interesting, not obvious.

### Newsletter Article (LinkedIn Newsletter)
Best for: long-form thought leadership, deep dives.
Use sparingly. LinkedIn notifies all subscribers — powerful but don't abuse it.

## Founder-Led vs Company Page

### Founder Personal Account (Primary)
- Gets 5-10x the organic reach of company pages
- Feels human, builds trust, drives real engagement
- Best for: opinions, stories, lessons, hot takes, build-in-public, hiring
- Post cadence: 3-5x/week minimum

### Company Page (Supporting)
- Lower organic reach but builds brand credibility
- Best for: product announcements, customer case studies, team culture, job posts, industry reports
- Post cadence: 2-3x/week
- Strategy: Reshare/amplify founder posts, share content the founder tags
- Company page should have its own voice — professional but not sterile

### Synergy Pattern
1. Founder posts hot take or story → company page reshares with added context
2. Company page posts case study → founder shares with personal commentary
3. Founder announces feature personally → company page follows up with details/docs

## Anti-Patterns (What NOT to Do)

1. **Corporate jargon.** "We're excited to announce our synergistic solution..." → Nobody reads past line 1.
2. **Link-in-comments spam.** If you must share a link, put it where it naturally fits. The "link in comments" trick is tired.
3. **Engagement bait without substance.** "Agree? 👍" with no real content. Algorithm penalizes, audience resents.
4. **Overly polished / clearly AI-written.** If it sounds like ChatGPT wrote it, people scroll past.
5. **Only BOFU content.** Constant product pitches without value = unfollowed.
6. **Inconsistency.** Posting 5x one week then disappearing for a month destroys momentum.
7. **Hashtag stuffing.** 1-3 relevant hashtags max. #MondayMotivation #Entrepreneurship #Leadership is a red flag.
8. **No engagement.** Posting and ghosting. You must reply to comments within 1-2 hours of posting.
9. **Generic motivational content.** "Success is a journey, not a destination" → adds zero value.
10. **Copying someone else's style wholesale.** Adapt patterns, don't copy voices.
