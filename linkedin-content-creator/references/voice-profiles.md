# Voice Profiles

Author-specific voice context for LinkedIn content. Update this file as new authors are onboarded.

## Everest Guerra — Founder, Stitchi

**Source:** Analysis of 4 LinkedIn posts (origin story, SEO failure, year-end reflections, website relaunch).

### Voice Characteristics
- **Tone:** Personal, professional, and useful. Conversational but not sloppy.
- **Perspective:** Operator in the trenches — shares what he's learning, building, and occasionally getting wrong.
- **Storytelling:** Narrative-driven. Starts with a hook ("We spent the last nine months executing a SEO strategy, and it failed"), then walks through the story with specific details, names, and numbers.
- **Vulnerability:** Willing to share failures in detail (SEO post), pivots (self-serve → service model), and unexpected turns (layoff → full-time venture). This is a strength — lean into it.
- **Specificity:** Uses concrete details — "61 human-written articles," "8,000 pairs of custom sweatpants," "$70,000 saved," "MOZ domain authority of 13." Never vague.
- **Credibility signals:** Morning Brew connection (where Stitchi was born), University of Michigan, Greek Dressing exit. Mentioned naturally as context.
- **Team and community:** Tags and credits team members, advisors, and collaborators. Generous with attribution.
- **Structure:** Tends toward longer, structured posts. Uses numbered lists for analysis. Good use of whitespace.
- **What he does NOT do:** Humble-brag, use buzzwords, post generic motivation, or write one-liner hot takes.

### Voice Summary
Everest writes like a thoughtful founder who respects his audience's time. He tells real stories with real details, shares lessons from failures as openly as wins, and credits the people around him. His posts read as if a smart friend is telling you what happened over coffee — structured enough to be useful, personal enough to be interesting.

### Sample Hooks (from real posts)
- "We spent the last nine months executing a SEO strategy, and it failed."
- "'Why did you start Stitchi?' I get this question a lot."
- "4 years later… a big update!"
- "Where to even begin?"
- "Ever wonder what it really takes to build a business from the ground up?"

## Stitchi Company Page

**Inspiration:** beehiiv company page (playful, personality-driven, not corporate).

### Voice Characteristics
- **Tone:** Warm, witty, professional but not stiff. Has personality.
- **Distinct from founder:** Lighter, more playful. The founder goes deep; the company page is the approachable brand layer.
- **Content types:** Product announcements, customer spotlights, team culture, industry tips, curated content.
- **Format tendency:** Shorter posts. Can be punchy one-liners, image + caption, or short case study summaries.
- **Avoid:** Press release language, generic corporate voice, posting only product pitches.

## Adding a New Author

When a new person requests a post and no profile exists here:

1. Ask: "Who is the author of this post? Can you share their LinkedIn profile or a few past posts so I can match their voice?"
2. Analyze their existing content for: tone, sentence length, storytelling style, vulnerability level, use of humor, typical hooks, and formatting patterns.
3. Write a voice profile following the format above.
4. Append it to this file for future use.

If the user can't provide samples, ask these 5 questions:
1. How would you describe your writing voice in 3 words?
2. Are you comfortable sharing failures/challenges, or prefer wins-focused content?
3. Do you prefer short punchy posts or longer narrative-style posts?
4. What's your role and what do you want to be known for on LinkedIn?
5. Any posts by other people whose style you admire?
